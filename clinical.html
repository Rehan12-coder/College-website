Clinical Training Program

Hands-on experience in diverse healthcare settings

Clinical Education

Integrating classroom knowledge with real-world healthcare experience

Our Approach

We believe clinical education is the cornerstone of nursing preparation. Our program provides progressive clinical experiences that build from fundamental skills to complex patient care, ensuring graduates are practice-ready.

Supervised Learning

All clinical experiences are supervised by qualified faculty and clinical instructors who provide guidance, feedback, and evaluation. Our low student-to-faculty ratio ensures personalized attention during clinical training.

Competency Development

Our clinical program focuses on developing essential nursing competencies including clinical judgment, technical skills, communication, collaboration, and professional behaviors.

Clinical Placement Sites

We partner with leading healthcare institutions for comprehensive clinical training

Bhakkar Teaching Hospital

Primary clinical site with diverse patient population across medical, surgical, pediatric, obstetric, and emergency departments.

Regional Cardiac Center

Specialized rotations in cardiac care, intensive care units, and cardiology departments for advanced clinical experience.

Community Health Centers

Community-based experiences in rural and urban health centers, schools, and public health programs.

Mental Health Facility

Specialized psychiatric nursing experiences in inpatient and outpatient mental health settings.

Maternal & Child Health Center

Comprehensive experience in labor/delivery, postpartum care, nursery, and pediatric units.

Primary Care Clinics

Ambulatory care experiences in family practice, internal medicine, and specialty clinics.

Clinical Rotation Structure

Progressive clinical experiences throughout the nursing program

Year 1: Foundations

  • Basic nursing skills practice
  • Health assessment practice
  • Introduction to clinical environment
  • Long-term care facility experience
  • Communication skills development

Year 2: Medical-Surgical Focus

  • Adult medical-surgical nursing
  • Pharmacology administration
  • Documentation and care planning
  • Beginning leadership skills
  • Specialty department introductions

Year 3: Specialty Rotations

  • Maternal-child health nursing
  • Pediatric nursing
  • Mental health nursing
  • Community health nursing
  • Critical care introduction

Year 4: Advanced Practice

  • Complex patient care management
  • Preceptorship experience
  • Leadership and management
  • Emergency and critical care
  • Transition to practice

Simulation Equipment

Faculty Support

Our experienced faculty guide students through simulation exercises, provide real-time feedback, and facilitate debriefing sessions to maximize learning outcomes.

Student Benefits

  • Safe environment to practice and make mistakes
  • Increased confidence before entering real clinical settings
  • Opportunities to repeat and master essential skills
  • Exposure to rare or complex clinical situations

Access & Scheduling

Simulation labs are available throughout the academic year. Students are scheduled for regular sessions and may request additional practice time as needed.
